2017 EIA Energy Conference

June 26, 2017 @ 8:00 am - June 27, 2017 @ 5:00 pm

  • « 2016 Health Datapalooza session: Data-Driven Innovations for Mental Health, Invisible Illness, and Suicide Prevention
  • 2017 DATA Act Summit »

The U.S. Energy Information Administration’s 2017 Energy Conference will be held June 26–27 at the Washington Hilton Hotel at 1919 Connecticut Avenue, NW, Washington DC. I want to make sure you don’t miss this opportunity to learn firsthand about the current trends and key developments affecting energy at the state, national, and global level.

Session topics include:

  • The future of U.S. nuclear power
  • Petroleum exports: Competing in the global market
  • Coal–natural gas competition: The current state of play
  • Global transportation, electric vehicles, and fuel demand
  • Gasoline fuel quality: The looming octane shortage
  • Renewable project financing: Current and future
  • The energy-water nexus and induced seismicity
  • Natural gas infrastructure to serve growing markets
  • Human behavior and energy use in buildings
  • Electrification in developing countries
  • “Big Data” and energy information
  • Energy data transparency

Keynote speakers include:

  • Greg Walden, Chairman of the U.S. House Energy and Commerce Committee
  • Alex Laskey, President and Founder, Opower
  • Scott Sheffield, Executive Chairman of the Board at Pioneer Natural Resources
  • Dr. Sun Xiansheng, Secretary General, International Energy Forum

Register today at: https://www.eia.gov/conference/2017/

Online registration closes at noon EDT on Friday, June 23.

For questions email: tina@fbcinc.com
